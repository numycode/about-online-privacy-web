Part I: The Myths

To start, privacy is not the thing you are thinking of. You are actually thinking of secrecy. According to privacyguides.org,
"This is a dangerous misconception, because it creates a sense that people who demand privacy must be
deviant, criminal, or wrong," and, "You shouldn't confuse privacy with secrecy.
We know what happens in the bathroom, but you still close the door. That's because you want privacy, not secrecy."

For the second myth, all Incognito mode does is it doesn't save your search history or cookies in your browser.

That's it.

Google can still track you (I'll dive more into that in a moment), your ISP can still see what you search.
That reminds me!

VPNs. They are advertised as the only thing you need for online privacy. But do they really do that?
The main, actually helpful thing they can do is change your location, which can help you access geo-fenced content
(which means there is a system in place where if it detects you are a country, it blocks/allows content.)
This could, for example, let you access shows that you can only watch in the EU.

But then you ask, with a slighly differed point of view,
"How much data ACTUALLY leaks when you don't take your privacy seriouly, then?"
